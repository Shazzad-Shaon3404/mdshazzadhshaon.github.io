About

Md. Shazzad Hossain Shaon is a Ph.D. student in the Department of Computer Science and Engineering at Oakland University (OU), Michigan, USA. He earned his Bachelor of Science in Computer Science and Engineering from Daffodil International University, Bangladesh, where he graduated with distinction and built a strong foundation in software engineering, algorithms, and data-driven applications. At Oakland University, Shaon‚Äôs research lies at the intersection of artificial intelligence, cybersecurity, and health informatics. His work focuses on designing AI-driven security frameworks and applying large language models (LLMs), machine learning (ML), and deep learning (DL) to develop innovative and secure solutions for data-driven healthcare applications. He is particularly interested in advancing AI-based vulnerability detection, natural language processing (NLP), and algorithmic approaches that enhance both data security and clinical decision-making. Shaon is also a recipient of funding from the National Aeronautics and Space Administration (NASA), through the Michigan Space Grant Consortium (MSGC). In this NASA-funded project, he explores AI-based methods for vulnerability detection and software security in aerospace systems. In addition to his doctoral research, Shaon has published papers in prestigious peer-reviewed journals and leading international conferences, showcasing his contributions to AI security, LLMs for code analysis, and healthcare informatics. He continues to collaborate with faculty and interdisciplinary teams on projects that integrate state-of-the-art AI methods with real-world healthcare and aerospace challenges.
